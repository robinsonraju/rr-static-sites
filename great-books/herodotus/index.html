Herodotus: The History

Explore the nine books of the Inquiries (Histories)

Book I

Clio

Covers the rise of the Lydian kingdom under Croesus, the Persian conquest of Lydia, and the early reign of Cyrus the Great.

Explore →

Book II

Euterpe

A detailed geographical, cultural, and historical survey of Egypt, focusing on its rulers and unique customs.

Explore →

Book III

Thalia

Details the reign of Cambyses, the conquest of Egypt, the death of the Magian, and the accession of Darius I.

Explore →

Book IV

Melpomene

Focuses on Darius's expedition against the Scythians and a description of Libya and its various peoples.

Explore →

Book V

Terpsichore

The Ionian Revolt Begins. Covers the conquest of Thrace, the murder of Persian envoys in Macedon, and the events leading up to the burning of Sardis.

Explore →

Book VI

Erato

Details the end of the Ionian Revolt and the first Persian invasion of Greece, culminating in the Battle of Marathon.

Explore →

Book VII

Polymnia

Focuses entirely on Xerxes' preparations and the massive Persian invasion, ending with the Battle of Thermopylae.

Explore →

Book VIII

Urania

Covers the sea battles of Artemisium and the decisive Greek victory at the Battle of Salamis.

Explore →

Book IX

Calliope

Concludes the history with the Greek victories at Plataea and Mycale, marking the end of the Persian invasion.
